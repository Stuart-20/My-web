Uganda is often called the " Pearl of Africa" because of the it's water bodies offer breathtaking natural beauty and
unforgettable experiences for travelers From the mighty River Nile , the world’s longest river, to the vast Lake Victoria ,
Africa’s largest freshwater lake,
Uganda’s aquatic treasures attract thousands of tourists annually.

Lake Victoria

This is largest lake in Africa and the second-largest freshwater lake in the world. It is shared by Uganda, Kenya, and Tanzanai. Covering over 68,800 square kilometers
In Uganda it touches districts like Entebbe, Jinja, Kalangala, Mukono, and Masaka

The lake is the main source of mighty River Nile. A Monument at the source of the Nile marks this Historical point. The Lake is popular for boat cruises, bird watching, and scenic views.
It has numerous Islands like Ssese Islands which are key tourist spots, Home to over 200 fish species, Rich birds around the shores

River Nile

The River Nile, the longest on Earth (world), begins its majestic journey of about 6,650km right here in Uganda, flowing north from Lake Victoria at jinja.
This section of the Nile offers not only stunning natural beauty but also some of Uganda's top adventure and tourism experiences.
The source was discoverd in 1862 by a British explorer John Hanning Speke on L.victoria in Jinja, Uganda making it the most famous tourism hub on the river
